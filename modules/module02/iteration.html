Iteration

Programming Repeated Steps

it•er•a•tion (ˌɪt əˈreɪ ʃən)

n.

  1. the act of repeating; a repetition.
  2. a problem-solving or computational method in which a succession of approximations, each building on the one preceding, is used to achieve a desired degree of accuracy.

Loops

You often here iteration referred to as a loop because of the way it modifies the behavior of your program:

Why Iteration?

Imagine you want to print all the positive integers less than 10. You wouldn’t really want to do this:

print(1)
print(2)
print(3)
print(4)
print(5)
print(6)
print(7)
print(8)
print(9)

While Loop

Instead you could use a while loop with a variable counter. This is very useful if you want to print all the positive integers less than 1 million…

i = 0
while i < 1000000:
    i += 1
    print(i)
